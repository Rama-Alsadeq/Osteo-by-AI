Welcome to Osteo by AI

Osteo by AI is an intelligent diagnostic platform for detecting Osteoarthritis (OA) and Osteoporosis (OP). It integrates knee X-ray medical imaging and clinical data into a unified AI framework, delivering accurate, real-time support for clinicians and patients.

Who Can Use This Tool?

This platform is accessible to everyone: individuals who are concerned and want to know if they should consult a doctor, as well as healthcare professionals seeking AI-assisted support in clinical decision-making.

Note: This tool is designed to support clinical decision-making and should not be considered a full replacement for professional medical advice or diagnosis.

Osteoarthritis (OA)

Osteoarthritis is the most common musculoskeletal disorder worldwide. It is a progressive degenerative joint disease characterized by cartilage degradation, subchondral bone changes and osteophyte formation, most often affecting knees, hips and hands. OA causes pain, stiffness and reduced mobility, and imposes a large clinical and economic burden.

Diagnosis

Early OA is difficult to detect on plain radiography because cartilage loss is not directly visible and X-rays provide 2D projections of a 3D joint. MRI is more sensitive but costly and less available. Standard radiographic grading (Kellgren–Lawrence) remains the most widely used clinical tool for X-ray based assessment. In this project, we developed an automated system that diagnoses knee osteoarthritis from X-ray images based on the Kellgren–Lawrence scale, classifying the disease into three categories. The model achieved high accuracy, demonstrating its potential to support early and reliable OA assessment in clinical practice.

Osteoporosis (OP)

Osteoporosis is a progressive skeletal disease characterized by low bone mass and deterioration of bone microarchitecture, leading to increased bone fragility and fracture risk. It is often asymptomatic until the first fragility fracture occurs, which makes screening and risk assessment crucial.

Diagnosis

Osteoporosis is commonly diagnosed using Dual-energy X-ray Absorptiometry (DXA), which measures Bone Mineral Density (BMD) and provides T-score categories (normal, osteopenia, osteoporosis). In our project, we extended this concept by providing both T and Z scores directly for easier diagnosis. We also developed two AI models: one based on knee X-ray images and another based on clinical risk factors. Finally, we built an ensemble model that combines DXA-like scoring with both AI models, offering a more comprehensive and direct approach for osteoporosis assessment.

Osteoarthritis (OA) Model Analysis

In this project, we focused on diagnosing knee osteoarthritis using standard X-ray images. The diagnosis follows the internationally recognized Kellgren–Lawrence (KL) grading system, which classifies the disease into grades 0–3, based on visible changes in the joint. Our system uses artificial intelligence to first locate the knee joint in the image, then classify the severity of osteoarthritis, and finally provide an explanation of the result by highlighting the affected regions.

How It Works

  • The system first identifies and isolates the knee joint area from the X-ray (YOLO-based joint localization).
  • Then, it analyzes the joint using pre-trained smart models (transfer-learning EfficientNetB3 ) , which is trained to recognize the Kellgren–Lawrence grades (0–3).
  • Finally, the model provides a visual explanation by showing which parts of the X-ray influenced the decision most (Grad-CAM heatmaps), such as areas with joint space narrowing, bone spurs, or sclerosis.

Why It Matters

  • The system achieved a classification accuracy of 93%, meaning it can reliably support clinical decisions.
  • It showed a very low missed-case rate (< 2%), which increases confidence in early detection.
  • This tool is valuable for early diagnosis of osteoarthritis. It can help doctors prioritize patients who may need more advanced imaging (MRI) or referral to specialists for treatment and rehabilitation.

Osteoporosis (OP) Model Analysis

This platform provides an indicative assessment of osteoporosis risk. It combines three approaches: AI analysis of knee X-rays (radiographs), clinical risk factors, and definitive Bone Mineral Density results (T-score & Z-score from DEXA) when available. Together, these help prioritize patients for further testing and early intervention.

  • X-ray AI model: evaluates bone quality and early fracture signs from knee images.
  • Clinical risk AI model: considers age, sex, BMI, medications, and other health factors.
  • BMD/DEXA: provides the definitive T-score and Z-score for diagnosis.
  • Ensemble model: integrates all three sources for more reliable risk estimation.

Methodology

  • AI model for knee X-rays using transfer learning (EfficientNetB3)
  • AI model for clinical risk factors using Gradient Boosting
  • Ensemble model combining all three sources: BMD/DEXA 50% – Risk AI 30% – X-ray AI 20%
  • Explainable outputs with Grad-CAM visualizations for X-ray interpretation

Clinical Insights

  • Supports early detection and prioritization for DXA testing.
  • Helps identify patients at risk for fractures.
  • Accuracy of individual models: X-ray AI model 81% (3-class), Clinical Risk AI model 91% (2-class: disease vs. no disease).
  • If one model provides an inaccurate assessment, the other models can offer a more accurate evaluation. Each of the three models (BMD, Risk Factors, and X-ray imaging) is also capable of functioning independently.

General Key Features

  • ✔ Rapid, automated X-ray and clinical data analysis.
  • ✔ Explainable results with visual Grad-CAM heatmaps.
  • ✔ Reduced diagnostic errors through ensemble modeling.
  • ✔ Accessible design for use in clinics with limited resources.

Future Outlook

Future work includes integrating local clinical datasets, expanding diagnostic coverage to other musculoskeletal disorders, improving model calibration over time, and moving towards real-world clinical validation and deployment. Osteo by AI aims to be a reliable assistant in modern healthcare.
